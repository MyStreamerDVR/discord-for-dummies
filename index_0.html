THE TEN DEGREES OF DISCORD

1: Gaslight Guardian

Frame their passion as pathology. Attack their emotional stability, not their point. Make them defend their own state of mind, an unwinnable battle.

2: Semantic Executioner

Ignore the argument entirely. Find a grammatical error, a typo, or a misused word. Make that the entire focus. It's not about being right; it's about making them feel incompetent.

3: The 'Curiosity' Cobra

Wrap your most poisonous accusation in the form of an innocent question. Force them to disprove a negative while you maintain plausible deniability as a "humble truth-seeker."

4: Willpower DoS Attack

A.K.A. "Sealioning". Become a machine of polite, endless requests for evidence on self-evident claims. The goal is total exhaustion, not debate. They will capitulate to make you go away.

5: The Poisoned Echo

Agree with them, but amplify their position to a monstrous, untenable extreme. This forces them to argue against a caricature of their own belief, making them look foolish and inconsistent.

6: In-Group Inquisitor

Attack them from within their own tribe. Question their purity, their credentials, their dedication. Cast them as a "poser" or "casual." The fear of exile from one's own group is a powerful weapon.

7: Archaeologist's Shiv

Dig up their post history. Find a contradictory statement, no matter how old. Expose them as a hypocrite by weaponizing their past self against their present self.

8: Moral Kaleidoscope

Isolate the moral principle behind their argument. Apply that same principle to a different context where it makes them look monstrous or foolish. Trap them in their own rigid virtue.

9: The Gentle Misquote

Rephrase their argument, but change one critical word that subtly alters its meaning into something weaker or more extreme. "So what you're saying is..." is the scalpel. They are now forced to clarify, putting them on the defensive and making them seem incoherent.

10: The unsolvable Koan

Respond to a complex argument with a short, philosophical, and completely unrelated question. This derails the entire logical track of the conversation and makes the original poster seem small-minded for caring so much about their "trivial" point.
